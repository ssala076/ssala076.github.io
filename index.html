Systolic Blood Pressure

You can calculate your systolic blood pressure by multiplying your age by 5 and adding 100. For example, if you're 50 years old, multiply it by 5 to get 250 and add 100 to get 350. This is the average maximum pressure that your blood places on the walls of your blood vessels.
